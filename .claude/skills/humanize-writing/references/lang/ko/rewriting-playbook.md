# Rewriting playbook — Korean (한국어)

Concrete fix recipes for each Korean AI-tell category. The detector says *where*; this says *how*.
Always apply the **smallest** edit that kills the tell. Read alongside `ko/ai-tell-taxonomy.md`.

## General order
1. Remove S1s first (each alone outs the text).
2. Thin S2 clusters to 1–2 occurrences.
3. Leave lone S3s unless three or more pile up.
4. After editing, **read aloud in Korean.** If it sounds like a news anchor reading a press release, you've introduced KO-E.

---

## KO-A 어휘 (vocabulary)

- **다양한**: delete entirely when modifying a noun that speaks for itself. "다양한 기능" → "기능". If variety genuinely matters, be specific: "열 가지 기능".
- **효과적인 / 중요한 / 필요한**: cut unless the adjective adds real information the noun doesn't carry. "효과적인 방법" → "방법" (or name the method).
- **활용하다**: → 쓰다 / 사용하다. Reserve 활용 for when "making full use of" is the actual point.
- **향상시키다**: → 높이다 / 나아지다 / 늘리다 depending on what improves.
- **구현하다**: → 만들다 / 넣다 / 적용하다 depending on context.
- **제공하다**: → 주다 / 내놓다. Only keep 제공 in formal/legal register where it belongs.
- Never swap one formal word for another formal word — replace with the spoken-register word.

## KO-B 번역체 도입부 (translationese openers)

- Delete the opener entirely. The sentence that follows is the real start.
- "오늘날의 빠르게 변화하는 세상에서, 우리는…" → "우리는…"
- "현대 사회에서 X는 중요합니다" → "X는 [구체적인 이유] 때문에 중요합니다" or just the reason itself.
- If the opener was providing context, fold that context into the first real sentence instead.

## KO-C 부정 대조 구조 (antithesis)

- Pick the one true claim and state it directly.
- "단순한 도구가 아니라 철학입니다" → "이 도구는 [구체적으로 어떻게] 사고방식을 바꿉니다"
- "X뿐만 아니라 Y도" used once is fine. Used in every paragraph → reduce to the one that matters most.
- Never keep the antithesis construction as the primary rhetorical move.

## KO-D 접속어 (connectives)

- 또한 → delete (the next sentence usually stands on its own) or → "그리고" / "게다가"
- 따라서 → "그래서" or restructure the sentence so the conclusion is implicit
- 첫째/둘째/셋째 stacked → convert to prose flow or use 먼저/다음/마지막으로 once only
- "이처럼" / "이를 통해" → delete; trust the reader to see the connection
- After deleting connectives: read the passage. If logic is unclear, restructure the sentence, don't re-add the connector.

## KO-E 어미 과잉 격식체 (formal endings in wrong register)

- Identify the target audience and their expected register (독자가 누구인가?).
- General/informal audience: mix ~요 endings naturally. "중요합니다" → "중요해요" or "중요합니다" once, not every sentence.
- "~하는 것이 중요합니다" → "~해야 합니다" → "~하세요" or just the imperative "~하십시오" if that fits, or simply restructure: "X가 성패를 가릅니다."
- Nominalization trap: "~하는 것을 고려해 볼 필요가 있습니다" → "~을 고려하세요" or "~을 고려하면 좋습니다"
- Vary sentence endings: declarative → question → short command. Not every sentence ~습니다.

## KO-F 결론 상투어 (conclusion tells)

- Delete "결론적으로" / "정리하면" / "요약하자면" — end on the last real point.
- "이것이 바로 X가 중요한 이유입니다" → delete; the argument already made the case.
- If a summary is genuinely needed (long document), write a crisp one-sentence takeaway without announcing it's a summary.

---

## Change-rate discipline

Same as English playbook: **>30%** → warn. **>50%** → stop and ask.

Korean prose often sounds more natural with shorter sentences than English. Don't mistake "shorter" for "over-edited" — varied rhythm is the goal, not brevity for its own sake.

## Failure modes to avoid

- *조사/어미만 바꾸기:* swapping 합니다 → 해요 across the board without improving the actual vocabulary or structure. Change the substance, not just the surface register.
- *번역체 → 다른 번역체:* replacing English-calque sentences with Chinese-calque compounds. Stay in natural spoken Korean.
- *과수정:* making every sentence three words. Korean allows long, flowing sentences — use them where they work.
