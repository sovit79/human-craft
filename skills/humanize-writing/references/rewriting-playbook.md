# Rewriting playbook (writing)

Concrete, copy-pasteable fix recipes for each taxonomy category. The detector says *where*; this
says *how*. Always apply the **smallest** edit that kills the tell.

## General order of operations
1. Remove S1s first (each alone outs the text).
2. Thin S2 clusters down to 1–2 occurrences.
3. Leave lone S3s unless three or more pile up.
4. After editing, **read it aloud.** If the rhythm is flat, you've introduced W-H.

---

## W-A vocabulary
- Replace with the spoken-register word: `delve` → "dig into / get into"; `leverage` → "use";
  `utilize` → "use"; `robust` → "solid"; `facilitate` → "help"; `myriad` → "many"; `pivotal` →
  "key" (or cut). Empty adjective + strong noun → drop the adjective.
- Don't trade one fancy word for another fancy word — that's the most common failed fix.

## W-B openers
- Delete the runway phrase; start on the first real idea. Test: if the sentence still works without
  its opener, the opener was filler.

## W-C "not just X, it's Y"
- `"It's not just a tool, it's a movement."` → pick the true half: `"It's a tool people rally
  around."` Or merge: `"It's a useful tool with a community behind it."` Never keep the antithesis.

## W-D rule of three
- Cut a triple to two, or expand to four, or replace with one strong item. Vary across paragraphs so
  no rhythm repeats.

## W-E connectives
- Delete sentence-initial `Moreover/Furthermore/Additionally` — meaning rarely changes. Where a link
  is needed, prefer "and / but / so" or start fresh.

## W-F balance/hedging
- Pick a side. `"There are pros and cons."` → say which you'd choose and why. Keep one genuine
  hedge only where uncertainty is real.

## W-G conclusions
- Cut "In conclusion / In summary / Ultimately." Often delete the whole summary sentence — let the
  last real point be the ending.

## W-H rhythm (the important one)
- Deliberately vary sentence length: follow a long sentence with a 3–5 word one. Add a fragment.
  Start consecutive sentences with different structures. Target a sentence-length coefficient of
  variation that looks human (varied), not a flat mean.

## W-I structure/formatting
- Convert a bulleted trio of full sentences back into prose. Reduce heading density. Use
  "first / then / finally" or nothing instead of "Firstly / Secondly / Thirdly."

## W-J ornamentation
- Strip decorative emoji from headers/bullets. Reduce em-dashes to one per few paragraphs; replace
  the rest with commas, periods, or parentheses. Bold only true labels/defined terms.

## W-K assistant voice
- Delete "Absolutely! / Great question! / I'd be happy to / Let's dive in!" and stray exclamation
  marks. Authored prose doesn't talk to the reader like a chat reply.

## W-L absence tells (add, don't just remove)
- Add one concrete specific the model wouldn't invent: a real number, a named example, a small true
  anecdote. Use contractions where you'd speak them. Let a real opinion show once.

---

## Change-rate discipline
- Track edited characters / total. **>30%** → warn the user the voice may have shifted. **>50%** →
  stop; this is a rewrite, not a de-slop — ask whether they want that.
- If a fix would alter meaning to remove a tell, **keep the meaning and accept the tell**, and note it.

## Failure modes to avoid
- *Synonym-swap slop:* replacing AI words with thesaurus words. Use plain speech instead.
- *Flattening:* over-editing until every sentence is the same short length (that's a new W-H).
- *Genre drift:* making a technical doc chatty. Respect register.
