# Ethics & responsible use

Human Craft is a **craft and quality** tool. Read this before using it anywhere disclosure matters.

## What it's for
- Removing the generic "AI default" so your writing and design read as deliberate, owned work.
- Learning *why* something reads as machine-made, so you write/design better unaided over time.
- Improving accessibility and clarity along the way (the design skill fixes real WCAG issues).

## What it is NOT for
- **Not an "Undetectable AI" / detector-bypass service.** The goal is quality, not evading tools.
  We make no claim about any detector, and we don't optimize against one.
- **Not for hiding AI use where disclosure is required.** Academic submissions, journalism, legal
  filings, regulated claims, and any context with a disclosure policy: **disclose your AI use.**
  De-slopping the prose doesn't change that obligation.
- **Not for falsifying provenance.** The image skill *reports* C2PA / Content Credentials / EXIF;
  it never strips or fakes them. Misrepresenting an image's origin is out of scope.

## Disclosure norm
If a reader, grader, client, or platform would reasonably expect to know that AI helped produce
something, tell them. "Edited for clarity and voice with AI assistance" is honest and costs nothing.

## Why this matters even to you
The tells exist because the output was *average*. Fixing them is mostly the same work as making the
thing genuinely good: a real opinion, a concrete detail, a deliberate aesthetic, a working,
accessible interface. Used this way, the tool makes the work better — not just harder to attribute.

## Academic / professional caution
Some institutions now flag text that's been run through "humanizer" tools. Using Human Craft to
disguise undisclosed AI work in those settings can violate policy regardless of detector outcomes.
Don't. Use it on work you're allowed to use AI for, and disclose per the rules that apply to you.

## Taxonomies are open
The taxonomy files are free for research and education (MIT). If you study or teach how AI content
is recognized, use and cite them.
